French Dessert

Caramel cream

The caramel cream is a dessert cream topped with a layer of caramel. Caramel cream is ubiquitous in European restaurants and particularly in France.

Crêpes

A crêpe is a type of very thin pancake. Crêpes are usually of two types: sweet crêpes and savoury galettes. Crêpes are served with a variety of fillings, from the simplest with only sugar to flambéed crêpes Suzette or elaborate savoury galettes.

Eclair au chocolat

A éclair, formerly known as Duchess's Bread, is a pastry of French origin consisting of elongated puff pastry stuffed with pastry cream, with icing on the top.p>

Burnt cream

Crème brûlée also known as burnt cream or Trinity cream, is a dessert consisting of a rich custard base topped with a texturally contrasting layer of hardened caramelized sugar. It is normally served slightly chilled.

Chocolate mousse

The chocolate mousse is a dessert whose traditional composition includes at least chocolate and egg white, mounted on snow. It can sometimes be embellished with egg yolk, sugar or cream, spices or citrus zest.

Apple pie

Apple pie is a type of sweet pie, made from puff pastry or broken with minced apples. This pie can be eaten hot, warm or cold.

Lemon pie

Lemon pie is a sweet pie filled with lemon cream. It does not include any fruit. The cream is a mixture of eggs, sugar, lemon juice and lemon zest. Also called lemon curd, it is this cream that gives the pie its taste. The lemon pie is often complemented by a meringue and becomes a lemon meringue pie.

Mi-cuit

The mi-cuit or flowing heart cake is a type of chocolate cake. The mi-cuit is made thanks to a quick cooking that ensures a flowable heart and a cooked exterior.

Floating Island

A floating island is a dessert of French origin, consisting of meringue floating on crème anglaise. The meringues are prepared from whipped egg whites, sugar, and vanilla extract then quickly poached. The crème anglaise is prepared with the egg yolks, vanilla, and hot milk, briefly cooked.



Polish Dessert

Faworki

Chruściki known also as faworki, angel wings are a traditional sweet crisp pastry made out of dough that has been shaped into thin twisted ibbons, deep-fried and sprinkled with powdered sugar.

Babka

Babka is a slightly sweet yeast bread similar to Italian panettone that can be made with rum-soaked raisins, and iced or left plain.

Kremowki

a Polish type of cream pie, a variation of French mille-feuille . It is made of two layers of puff pastry, filled with whipped cream, creamy buttercream, vanilla pastry cream (custard cream) or sometimes egg white cream, and is usually sprinkled with powdered sugar. It was beloved cake of pope John Paul II.

Makowiec

Makowiec is a poppy seed strudel - a roll of sweet yeast bread with a sweet filling of poppy seed, popular cake for Christmas.

Mazurek

Mazurek is a flat Polish cake made with a non-yeast dough and topped with preserves, eaten mainly for Easter. It can come in different flavours but the most popular is chocolate one.

Pączek

Pączek is a large fried doughnut filled with marmalades and glazed with sugar, and it might be topped with candied orange peel.

Pascha

Pascha is a traditional Easter dish of North-Russian origin, prepared from curd or milk, cream, butter, usually with the addition of yolks, sugar, vanilla and other ingredients.

Sernik

Sernik is a Polish Cheesecake made with special kind of cheese called "twarog" that can be found only in Poland. Sernik is light and fluffy, it can have raisings or candied peel.

Szarlotka

Szarlotka is polish apple pie, however is different than American apple pie in both the crust and the filling and tends to be less sweet.

French Alcohol

Cognac

Cognac is a brandy, close to Armagnac, made from white grape, distilled in stills and aged in oak barrels produced in Charente.

Rosé

Rosé is the ultimate summer wine, a young, fresh and trendy wine ideal for aperitif and as an accompaniment to tapas. It adapts perfectly to what are the majority of our meals today, less structured, encompassing the world's cuisine and blends with almost all the dishes of modern day gastronomy.

Calvados

The calvados is the normand brandy. Distilled with cider and / or perry, it is an integral part of the Norman identity. It can be enjoyed from appetizer to dessert but blends perfectly with apples.

Bordeaux's wine

Bordeaux wine is one of the most used metonymies by the French. Bordeaux brings together all styles of wine in a wide range of reds, whites and rosés.

Martinique's rum

But France does not stop at the country, if you have the chance to travel overseas, Martinique in particular you will have many opportunities to enjoy a small glass of rum. Produced from cane-to-sugar it can be old, white or amber. You can drink it pure but also and perhaps especially in cocktails.

Breton's cider

It is unthinkable to eat a pancake or Breton pancake without a bowl of cider. Apple-based, raw or sweet but still low in alcohol, its golden robe, its fine bubbles and its fruity aroma make it the ideal table companion.

Champagne

Probably the best known French alcohol in the world, this wine of great occasions does not need to be presented anymore. Champagne is to be drunk rigorously fresh but never frozen, preferably in a tulip glass, ideal to allow the bubbles to evolve and preserve the aromas of evaporation too fast.

Pastis

Pastis is the Provencal aperitif par excellence. Produced from star anise, green anise and liquorice, it is particularly fresh and has a taste of holidays. If you are in Marseille, you must order a "Ricard, the true pastis of Marseille".

Fourgerolles' Kirsch

The Kirsch de Fougerolles, produced in Franche-Comté is the first brandy of stone fruit because the kirsch is produced from guignes, small cherries very sweet.



Polish Alcohol

Vodka

Vodka is is a clear distilled alcoholic beverage composed primarily of water and ethanol traditionally it is made by distilling the liquid from fermented cereal grains or potatoes. The most highly regarded clear Polish vodka brands must be Belvedere, Chopin, Luksusowa, Ostoya, Pan Tadeusz and Wyborowa

Żubrówka

Żubrówka is a type of Polish vodka, which contains a bison grass blade (Hierochloe odorata) in every bottle. The grass is sourced from the Białowieża Forest. Each blade of bison grass is hand-picked and dried under natural conditions.

Cydr

Cydr is a cider, an alcohol baverage made from fruit juice, most often from apples. Polish Cider contains at least 90% of apple juice (not concentrate!) and one of the most poo\pular is Cydr Lubelski.

Piwo

Piwo stands for beer. Poland has rich tradition of producing beer that is beeing brewed there for over a thousand years. Most popular types that can be found in Poland are Euro lagers, Pilsner, Baltic porters, Amber lager style and Bock styles.

Nalewka

Polish liqueurs is called 'nalewka'. Nalewka is alcoholic extracts from fruit, spices, flowers or herbs created by macerating or infusing those ingredients in alcohol, usually vodka or neutral spirits.

Śliwowica

Śliwowica, plum brandy is made in the vicinity of Łącko, near Nowy Sącz. Śliwowica is a very strong brandy,(average 70-80% of pure alcohol) with a very intensive, delicious fruit taste.

Miód Pitny

Mead (miód pitny) is a traditional Polish alcoholic beverage created by fermenting honey with water sometimes with various fruits, spices, grain. It has been part of Polish culinary tradition for over a thousand years.

Wino

Wino is simply wine. Medieval Times were golden era for polish winemaking that has ended rapidly with the Little Ice Age around the mid-16th century that brought harsher climatic condition. Today, thanks to globalization polish wine is experiencing a revival.

Żołądkowa

Żołądkowa is a herbal vodka from Poland, and the leading brand of Polmos-Lublin/Stock Polska since 1950. The name translates as "bitter vodka for the stomach." Żołądkowa Gorzka follows in a long Polish tradition of infused fruit and herb vodkas known as nalewka

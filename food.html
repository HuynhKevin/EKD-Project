French Food

Filters:

Oyster

Oysters are the second flagship product found at Christmas and New Year. This shell can be consumed hot, but enthusiasts prefer it generally raw and alive. Oysters can be enjoyed naturally or with a drizzle of lemon juice, vinegar or a shallot sauce.

Snails

The snails with parsley butter, commonly called "Burgundy snails" are cooked as the name suggests with a butter cream and parsley, they are presented in their shells and they are tasted with a small peak/p>

Mouclade charentaise

Bouchot mussels are cooked with shallots, garlic cloves, garnished bouquets, spices, eggs, cream and especially Pineau des Charentes (local alcohol).

Tartiflette

The tartiflette is a dish from Savoie, since it is a gratin based potatoes, bacon, onions and Reblochon, a Savoyard cheese. There is a variant called "croziflette", where the potatoes are replaced by small savoyard pasta made from buckwheat called "crozets"

Raclette

The raclette, imported in Savoy from Switzerland, is a dish made from cheese of the same name that is melted (traditionally over a wood fire, but today more often in small electric pans) and that one accompanied by potatoes, charcuterie (unless you are a vegetarian), onions and gherkins.

Quiche lorraine

The quiche is one of the most delicious culinary specialties of Lorraine. Covered with bacon and migaine, this salty pie is ideally consumed as a starter. This traditional French dish is served with a good green salad or endive.

Fondue

The Savoyard fondue is also a perfect cheese dish to spend a convivial moment in winter. In a small pot, three cheeses (Beaufort, Compté, Emmental) are melted and white wine is added. With the help of a specific spike, each guest soaks small pieces of bread. The tradition is that the one who drops his piece of bread has a pledge. So stay focused!

Gratin dauphinois

In the Grenoble area, another winter dish has become a must. This is the gratin Dauphinois. Traditionally it is made of sliced ​​potatoes, cream, milk and nutmeg. No cheese is added in the original recipe but it is possible that the one you serve contains some. This dish is so pleasing that everyone loves to cook it in their own way.

Ratatouille

In Provence, it is the dish of grandmothers. The ratatouille is usually composed of tomatoes, zucchini, peppers and aubergines ... It is so delicious that today most restaurateurs offer as a side dish with fish or meat.

Boeuf bourguignon

Beef Burgundy is a traditional family dish. These are pieces of beef stewed in red wine with root vegetables and mushrooms. This is a typical Burgundy dish, since Burgundy is a region famous for its cattle farms and red wines, hence the name of this dish.

Steak tartare

Typical dish of French bistrots, steak tartare is a preparation of raw beef or horse and coarsely chopped with a knife. Spices are added to the preparation and an egg on the top. Usually served with fries, this is a good alternative to traditional steak-fries.

Pot-au-feu

The pot-au-feu is the traditional French family dish par excellence. This is a beef dish that cooks for a long time and over very low heat in a broth of carrots, turnips and leeks, which considerably softened the meat.



Polish Food

Filters:

Karp

Is a carp fish dish eaten for Christmas Eve. Poles usually eat the fish fried in batter or breadcrumbs, served cold in a jelly, or simmered with sweet seasonings – the so-called Jewish carp recipe. At times the fish is also served with a typical gray gravy.

Barszcz

It’s a soup made from beets and is traditionally served on Christmas Eve with special dumplings called "uszka".

Schabowy

Schabowy is thinly cut slices of pork loin, chopped, coated in crumbs with breadcrumbs and fried in fat. Usually served with boiled potatoes and salad.

Śledź

There is not one proper way to serve herring in Poland. They eat it pickled, creamed, fried, baked. or as an appetizer known as 'rollmops'. Herring is featured throughout the year in Poland but especially at holiday times like Christmas Eve, known as wigilia, New Year's Eve celebrations, and on Shrove Tuesday before Lent.

Sielawa

Sielawa, en vendace is a popular fish in Masuria, a region of Poland where the cuisine is based mainly on fish. One of the most common methods of marinating sielawa is placing it in vinegar. Representative dishe of Masurian cuisine is fried sielawa in vinegar.

Pyry z Gzikiem

Pyry z Gzikiem is traditional dish from Poznań City (Ewa's hometown). It's simply boiled Potatoes with Cottage Cheese. This is far and away Poznań’s most famous dish. This is mostly served on Fridays as a simple, tasty meatless dish.

Rosół

Rosól is polish chicken soup or broth, served with thin, home-made filini pasta It is often served at Sunday lunch in Polish homes. It is a common home remedy for colds and the flu.

Bigos

Bigos, know also as a Hunter's Stew is a dish orginated from Medieval times. It's a dish made of pickled cabbage, sauerkraut, chopped meat, mushrooms and smoked sausage. Every family has their own secret recipe!

Chodnik

This classic Polish cold soup is staple food during a hot summer days. It can be thought as a summer variation of barszcz. It's made with beets, cucumbers, and yogurt. Its sweet-sour flavor is refreshing which makes it perfect tool to fight with heat.

Placki ziemniaczane

Placki ziemniaczane are simply potato pancakes, they're famous polish comfort food. Placki taste delicious when served with mushroom sauce!

Pierogi

Pierogi are a delicious Polish dumplings food. There are few kinds of this food which differ in a filling - meat, potato, fruit, cottage cheese are the most popular. Nowadays they come in so many flavours - you can find tofu, chocolate, pumpkin pierogi!

Ogorkowa

Ogorkowa is a traditional Polish cucumber soup consisting of grated pickled cucumbers, diced potatoes, a variety of root vegetables cooked on meat broth, and it is sometimes thickened with flour and sour cream
